2019

01/29 - Game Jam Competition

In SBGD's Sping 2019 semester's first event, SBGD will hold a game making competition where club members will work in teams of four to create a visual novel game within a few hours using Tom Rothamel's Ren'Py engine to create games. The teams will reflect real game development teams and will consist of a designer, a programmer, an artist and a writer. At the end of the event, the teams will present their games and the best game will be decided by a group of judges including Tom Rothamel and several Stony Brook University Computer Science professors. Students from the Creative Writing and Digital Arts department will be enlisted to be included in the teams.

2018

12/11 - Game Night

For the last GBM of the semester, SBGD held a game night to help the club de-stress for finals. The E-board and the club played games including the newly released Super Smash Bros. Ultimate, Marvel vs. Capcom Infinite, Mario Kart 8 Deluxe and many others.


12/4 - Time In The Game Industry with Mike Pellechia

Mike Pellechia, an alum of Stony Brook University, manager of the video game company Onslaught Studios and a member of the Long Island Game Developers speaks about his time and experience with the video game development industry. Mike spoke about his work as part of a game's programming team and discussed the game he was working on currently. Mike also showed and demonstrated several games he had worked on and he described the hiring process by the the Long Island Game Developers and how he was hired by the company.
