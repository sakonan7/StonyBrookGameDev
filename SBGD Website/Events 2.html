11/27 - Making Your First, Second or Future Games

Hosted by Peter Ly, Peter discusses the intricacies of designing game sequels and game series. In this meeting, Peter discussed the concepts of retooling main gameplay mechanics in sequels, diverging from main mechanics in sequels and using other games in the same genre to make innovative, new ideas. In his presentation he uses the games Megaman, Pokemon, Fire Emblem, X-COM and Flamberge, games that are part of a series as examples of games that innovate and improve on previous games in their series. He specifically discussed how game series like Megaman include new gameplay elements in each new game to bring in new ideas as well as bring in new challenges, how game series like Pokemon, a series of single player and competitive games, include new gameplay elements and mechanics to add to and change competitive gameplay with each new installment, and how games like Flamberge are based on previous games in the Real Time Strategy genre but diverge from them and became very unique and different.


11/13 - Character Design

Hosted by guest speaker Sammy Lui, Sammy teaches the basic necessities and bare bones of designing an iconic video game protagonist with 9 steps. Sammy demonstrated the 9 essential aspects of character design: finding character inspiration, designing the character's story and appearance, shape usage, figure and posing, recognizable silhouette, level of complexity, accessorization, color combinations and textures. He demonstrated the steps that can be taken from developing a character description into a character design and vice versa. He deconstructed the design intricacies of the protagonist of the game Persona 5 to demonstrate how character design elements can be derived from a story and how a story can inspire character design elements and inspire the design process. Afterwards, he held two drawing exercises with the club. The first exercise was to sketch the video game protagonist Sonic The Hedgehog using the 9 steps he discussed and the second exercise was to design an original character using the 9 steps and using a genre in mind.


11/06 - Ren'Py With Tom Rothamel

Tom Rothamel, a former alum of Stony Brook and creator and manager of the video game company Ren'Py, demonstrates his visual novel game engine, Ren'Py to the club. Tom discussed the process of creating his game engine and the types of games that can be made using it. He also showcased several games he made on his engine, Highway Blossoms, SC2VN, and Over The Hills And Far Away and demonstrated how to use his engine. Afterwards, he discussed his time in the game industry and how he started his own company.
